About

I am a physicist, educator, and academic administrator at Kettering University in Flint, Michigan, where I serve as the Founding Head of the School of Foundational Studies and as an Associate Professor of Physics. The School of Foundational Studies brings together mathematics, natural sciences, and liberal arts under a shared commitment to foundational learning for engineers — the kind of learning that builds not just technical skill, but durable habits of mind.

My research background is in experimental condensed matter physics, with a focus on magnetic nanoparticles and antiferromagnetic materials and their potential applications in non-volatile data storage and biomedical therapies, including magnetic fluid hyperthermia for cancer treatment. That work has been published in journals including Physical Review B, ACS Nano, Nanotechnology, and the Journal of Applied Physics, and has accumulated more than 1,300 citations. These days I spend more of my time on the educational side of physics — designing courses, thinking about how introductory education can do better by students, and exploring computational approaches to teaching physical modeling.

I came to Kettering in 2014 as an Assistant Professor after positions at the University of Texas at El Paso and Arkansas Tech University. I earned my Ph.D. in Condensed Matter Physics from Wayne State in 2008 and my B.S. in Engineering Physics from Eastern Michigan University in 2003.

The administrative role I now hold is the most complex work I have ever done. Building a school from the ground up has clarified a lot of my thinking about leadership. The short version is that authority is not the same as trust, and trust is built slowly, through presence and consistency, not through declarations. I write about some of this on my blog, Boundary Conditions.

Outside of work, I am involved in science outreach, including quantum workshops for high school students and semiconductor education in the community. I am an active member of the American Physical Society and currently serve as Chair of the Eastern Great Lakes Section. Most importantly, I am a husband to Jennifer and a father to Riley, who are the foundation of everything I do outside the office and a good deal of what motivates me inside it. I am also a lifelong Detroit sports fan, with a particular love for the Tigers.
